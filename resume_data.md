## [cite_start]Thesis [cite: 46]
* [cite_start]**A Comparison of Efficiency and Energy Consumption between Spray Nozzles System and Ultrasonic Mist Maker in Automatic Humidification Control Greenhouses.** [cite: 47]
* [cite_start]I conducted a research project to investigate and compare the efficiency and energy consumption of a spray nozzle system and an ultrasonic mist maker system in an automated humidity-controlled greenhouse. [cite: 48]
* [cite_start]Design and build a humidity control system using IoT technology with MCU Node ESP32 and sensors to record and monitor the temperature, humidity, electricity, and water consumption in the greenhouse. [cite: 49]
* [cite_start]The vapor pressure deficit based on leaf temperature (vpdL) is controlled between 0.8 - 1.2 kPa by adjusting the relative humidity of the greenhouses. [cite: 50]
* [cite_start]The greenhouse was simulated by cultivating 27 lettuce plants in a $60\times200$ cm planting bed, divided into three experiment setups: (1) Uncontrolled humidity greenhouse, (2) Automatic ultrasonic mist maker system, and (3) Automatic spray nozzle system. [cite: 51]
* [cite_start]The results of the study revealed that both automated humidity-control systems maintained the vpdL values within the range of 0.8-1.2 kPa. [cite: 52]
* [cite_start]The vpdL value decreased significantly from an average of 2.54 kPa in the uncontrolled humidity greenhouse to 1.02 kPa and 0.91 kPa in the ultrasonic mist maker system and spray nozzle systems, respectively. [cite: 53]
* [cite_start]Internal humidity distribution inside the greenhouses comparing the vpdL values at two different locations within each greenhouse system showed no statistically significant difference $(p-value>0.05)$. [cite: 54]
* [cite_start]Evaluating the performance using Mean Absolute Error (MAE) and Root Mean Square Error (RMSE). [cite: 55] [cite_start]This indicates that both humidity control systems yielded comparable performance. [cite: 56]
* [cite_start]Comparing the electric energy and water consumption of two automated humidity control systems, the ultrasonic misting system exhibited higher electricity consumption, In terms of water consumption, the ultrasonic mist maker system used more water. [cite: 57]
